§17 docs(take-note): STM32-vs-ESP32 API-layer report — companion doc to the smoke test

Adds the layer-stack debate report born during the Buoi_12.0 smoke test (which
is why it belongs next to it): So_sanh_tang_API_STM32_vs_ESP32.md + .docx
hand-off twin (md2docx bridge, clickable TOC + List of Figures injected at
build time) + fig_12_0_layer_towers.png — the "two towers" TikZ figure:
STM32 bare-metal-first vs ESP32 framework-first side by side, open/internal/
closed color coding, the SPL gravestone and the closed radio box standing
BESIDE the towers (figure-skill self-inspect, 3 rounds — the split-cell radio
box failed twice and was simplified per the FIGURE-STANDARD rule).

Contents, as requested by the user:
- The "raw + HAL is 80% right" position and the three places the other 20%
  lives (layers stack rather than replace; LL as a scalpel for hot paths).
- Table 1: STM32 layer stack per chip line — raw and HAL are the only two
  layers covering EVERY line, proving the strategy.
- Table 2: ESP32 layer stack per chip line — digital-peripheral registers are
  public in every TRM, radio registers exist in NO TRM; framework-first
  philosophy, groundwork for reading Espressif datasheets later.
- Table 3: public-vs-private across both vendors — including the industry
  rule "a radio on die means a closed corner, whatever the vendor" (STM32WB's
  BLE blob vs Espressif's libnet80211/libpp), toolchain openness contrast, and
  the Arduino-community factor.
- The esp32-open-mac dossier with dates (first blog 2023-12; RIOT Summit
  2024-09; 38C3 Hamburg 2024-12-27..30), scale, achievements/limits, and
  verified links.
